What is a cross-Product? ^4bpXZwuj

Lets think about two vectors v and w in 2d space ^WCIjVBy5

Area of this shape

Orientation ^nSdtJd2G

A cross-Product of two vectors is the area
of the shape that those two vectors create
including a negative sign for orientation
if its flipped ^1GyMMjpr

* This in a way can be described as a third vector
   who's length is the determinant of the matrix that 
   its column space is those two vectors, because the
   determinant is a measurement of shift in areas in space
   and whos direction obeys the right hand rules.

   This can be computed if we put the first column of a matrix
   as our basis coordinates and our two vectors are the other
   columns and proceeding to calculate the determinant of it ^tdokK5a3

3d space: ^sFCT528O

In a more intuitive way to think about 3d vectors:

 ^lN1LYjce

if we take a cross product in 2d we get the area of the shape S
if we apply this logic to 3d with 3 vectors we want
the area of that given cube!

but how do we do that?
we take the 2d cross product, and an orthogonal direction indicating
height and multiply its length by the area of S

This gives us a way to find an orthogonal vector that we needed
with a certain length that we also needed from the definition
of 3d 3 vector cross product ^8KNiZRW4
