Key Considerations
during Altimetry missions ^iUANqbNS

1. Satellite Altitude
The altitude cannot be too low (<500) because atmospheric drag degrades orbits
And it cannot be too high (>1500) because signal strength weakens.

2. Orbit Eccentricity
Small Eccentricity (Near-Circular Orbits): Ensures uniform altitude relative to Earth’s surface,
providing consistent measurement precision globally.
Irregular altitude variations would introduce errors in data.

3. Inclination
The tilt of the satellite’s orbit relative to the equator.
determines the latitudal coverage, lower tilts miss the polar regions while
higher tilts have near global coverage

4. Repeat Cycle (Repeatability)
Revisiting the same ground track periodically to build time series data

5. Repeat Cycle vs. Spatial Resolution
Frequent revisits (good for temporal monitoring) but widely spaced ground tracks (lower spatial detail).
 Closely spaced tracks (detailed maps) require longer repeat cycles (due to orbital mechanics).
Instead we can combine Various altimeter missions in order to derive consistent time series data. ^z9HmF9SN

Dynamic Ocean Topography
(DOT) ^6gE8TOfb

represents the deviation of the sea surface height (SSH) from the geoid
Typically ±1−2 meters, reflecting subtle but critical variations in sea level.
DOT = SSH − Geoid.

This is caused by Density differences in water due to Variations in water temperature and salinity 
alongside Wind-driven surface currents 
These processes create slopes in the sea surface, which drive ocean circulation. ^8zeUYBOK

While the equation appears simple, combining these datasets (Geoid and SSH)
is complex due to fundamental differences in their properties:

The geoid is defined everywhere on Earth.
Derived from spherical harmonic models which is less detailed but has global
coverage

SSH is observed only along satellite ground tracks
which is Discontinuous and localized compared to the geoid, but is higher resolution.
and is Absent over land ^4T0PzkuF

Global Approach ^iiWFyDkt

we want to Harmonize SSH and geoid data in
the spectral domain (spherical harmonics).
BUT SSH is undefined over land, creating gaps in global coverage.
so we Fill land regions with geoid values and
Apply smoothing filters to blend ocean SSH and land-filled geoid values. ^zFPSIKWy

Profile Approach ^R9qO0ESO

Preserve the high-resolution along-track
SSH data without gridding.
However SSH is filtered along 1D tracks,
while the geoid is filtered in 2D which will cause errors
so we apply a filter correction to reconcile 1D (SSH)
and 2D (geoid) filtering effects. ^wlniNgj8
