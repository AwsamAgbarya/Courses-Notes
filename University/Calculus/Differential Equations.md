Differential Equations ^KzhliL1O

Differential equations are often used
when its easier to describe the situation
with relative change than absolute amounts.

for example: population size ^cZYFpBc5

There are two types of Differential equations ^EiglZFOr

Ordinary Differential equations ^kecSLL0a

Partial Differential equations ^ZKeT3DpF

Differential equations that only take in
a single variables (Time) ^B2Q4ZVLh

Dealing with functions that have multiple
inputs ^3vd64joK

Take for example the force of gravity
Gravity of earth applies 9.8 m/s per second on objects that are
affected by it.

Meaning we know that the rate of change of velocity of an object is
9.8 m/s (Acceleration)

This is obviously assuming that the object being affected is insignificantly small
in comparsion to the planet where its mass and its own gravity do not
contribute much. this is however different for two planets affecting each other
the equations becomes inversely proportional to the distance between them
in the direction of the other, proportional to their mass as well. ^2lwUzIuR

This is a description of how a function evolves over time!

If the function has state x(t) at time t, what is the state x(t+dt)? ^9YOSTCam

In other words, given the derivative (rate of change), what is our function x(t)? ^6uXKRvTX
