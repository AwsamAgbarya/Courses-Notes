## Text Elements
Representations ^C7MJguq1

We need representations of the objects!

   1. Geometry
   2. Reflection behavior (color, shininess, ...)
   3. Light sources (intensity, direction)
   4. Textures (images on the surface of an object)
   
   * We tackle those issues in the upcoming notes! ^ei2MzdnG

But How do we sense this information?
 ^lL2KzvM8

Using a camera to sense optical properties 
of the real world we can obtain:

1. Intensity of light rays


2. Creating a depth map to simulate real life


 ^rfuyxZft

  Usually only sensing of projection on plane! 
 ^zu5tM5ZT

   Possibly fusing several depth maps ^xkesWzfC


What does a camera do?
Light rays pass through an optical center of the camera.
Optical properties of world are projected on plane
which is equipped with a light sensor that records
the amount of light that passed through
 ^2ZPxZZ36

What does the sensor do?

Contains Light sensitive elements that filter out light.
Consisting of only three primary colors because it is sufficient!
Usually has more green than anything else. ^GerSrqEm

How do we detect Geometry?
We use Triangulation! ^Vg2Y44Aw

Triangulation of Light and Camera Rays ^mMs7B4HK

Triangulation of Camera Rays ^8xco4mgV

Projecting ^uxoj4XJN

Sensing ^gKAXaipB

We Know the following from the setup:

. Distance vector of optical centers = Base
. Orientation of projection planes relative to base
 ^CE9f3KWg
